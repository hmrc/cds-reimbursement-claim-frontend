Claim back a security deposit or guarantee

You can use this service to claim back an import security deposit or guarantee you paid to HMRC for goods you brought into the UK to store, repair, process or temporarily use.

  • Contents
  •   What you’ll need
  •   Who can claim
  •   Types of claims
  •   Supporting documents examples
  •   What happens next
  •   If you need help

What you’ll need

Before you start, you must be subscribed to the Customs Declaration Service and have a Government Gateway user ID and a password linked to your EORI number.

To claim a security deposit or guarantee you’ll need:

  • an import and export Movement Reference Number (MRN) from the Customs Declaration Service
  • to know how the goods will be received — for example, imported as freight or post
  • details of your guarantee (if applicable)
  • to know how long the goods will be in UK
  • to know what you did with the goods
  • your bank details
  • your contact and address details
  • duty or VAT amount due and paid to HMRC
  • supporting documents

Who can claim

You can claim if you are an importer, a customs agent, freight forwarder or fast parcel operator, acting on behalf of the importer.

Types of claims

Temporary admission

A temporary admission authorisation allows you to import goods into the UK for a set period of time before re-exporting them.

Temporary admissions can be for 2, 3 or 6 months, or 2 years. You can submit a claim when the time period comes to an end and you no longer have the goods.

Inward processing

Inward processing allows you to import goods into the UK to process or repair them. To submit a claim, you have to tell HMRC how you disposed of the goods.

Authorised use (Great Britain) or end use (Northern Ireland)

You can pay less duty on some goods you import from outside the UK if they’re going to be processed or put to a specific use. You can submit a claim once the goods have been used as specified.

Supporting documents examples

Files can be up to 9MB each and can include:

  • commercial invoices
  • import or export declaration
  • packing lists
  • PIM/PIEX international consignment notes
  • sea or air waybill
  • bill of lading
  • international consignment note
  • customers order or sales contract
  • correspondence between companies
  • substitute or diversion entry
  • proof of authority to update bank account details
  • other documents that support your claim

What happens next

HMRC will review your claim. To find out more about the status of your claim, visit the claims dashboard. We will contact you to either:

  • confirm your claim and refund of deposit or guarantee
  • ask for more information
  • explain why your claim was rejected

If you need help

Find out more information about importing goods into the UK.

For general enquiries about importing, you can contact HMRC (opens in new tab).

Online services may be slow during busy times. Check if there are any problems with this service.
